Team Leader

Dr. James Mutua.

Career Overview

Dr. James Mutua is an early career researcher who is currently actively involved in numerous researches.
He is a specialist in Materials Engineering, Additive manufacturing, Design &Production, Recycling, and Lightweight materials.
Current research includes:
Composite material development, High performance materials engineering, Computational Materials Modelling, Recycling of waste plastics, and additive manufacture of metal materials and biomedical materials for Medicare.
He serve as the Chief Investigator who sphere heads the section in new material development, recycling of plastics and additive manufacturing of bio based energy devices.
He also serve as the key supervisor and mentor to all the postgraduate students and Junior staff involved in the research.
In addition, he is responsible for continuous monitoring and progress presentation to the Africa-ai-Japan Project phase 2 research and innovation grants office.

Concurrently serves as:

Lecturer Department of Mechanical Engineering, SOMMME, COETEC
Jomo Kenyatta University of Agriculture and Technology (JKUAT).

  1. Thematic leader of Team 1 who oversees Materials redesign, recycling, and additive manufacture of energy related devices.

  6. Obtain, distribute and manage all project funding for the various thematic areas as per budget.

Team Member

Dr. Japheth Obiko

Career Overview

Dr. Japheth Obiko (an expert in Metal Additive Manufacturing and Material Forming processes),
Dr Obiko has a PhD from the School of Chemical and Metallurgical Engineering, University of the Witwatersrand-Johannesburg, South Africa obtained in 2021.
He received an MSc in Mechanical Engineering from Jomo Kenyatta University of Agriculture and Technology in 2015 and BSc (Mechanical Engineering) from Jomo Kenyatta University of Agriculture and Technology, Kenya in 2012.
He is a registered graduate engineer with the Engineers Board of Kenya.
He is currently involved in different research topics focusing in areas of metal forming processes, welding metallurgy, material characterisation and alloy design and thermodynamic analysis.
He is also interested in both advanced and conventional machining processes of novel materials.
He is also interested in the application of Industry 4.0 technologies on welding and metal forming processes.
He has published several peer-reviewed articles in journals, book chapters and conferences.
He guides and mentors several undergraduate and postgraduate students in their research projects.
Dr Obiko is passionate about the adoption of Industry 4.0 in African manufacturing systems and as such, he is working towards expanding his research networks and experience in this topic.
He hopes to achieve this by working with various research groups around Africa/the world and fostering academia-industry relationships.

Concurrently serves as:

Lecturer in the Department of Mining, Materials and Petroleum Engineering ,SOMMME, COETEC
Jomo Kenyatta University of Agriculture and Technology (JKUAT).
